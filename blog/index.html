Breaking into Bioinformatics: My Journey and Advice

Published July 2025 · By Aarushi Gajri

When I started my undergraduate degree at IIT Mandi in Bioengineering, I had no idea I'd be presenting AI models for protein–ligand interaction at Georgia Tech a few years later. Like many students, I found bioinformatics through a mix of curiosity, coding, and a desire to understand life at the molecular level.

Step 1: Find your curiosity

It began with a fascination for how genes and mutations work. My exposure to genomic tools and biological data in class sparked the urge to explore further.

Step 2: Build your skills

I dove into Python, R, bash, and later PyTorch and deep learning. My internships at Elucidata, UBC, and MedGenome gave me real-world exposure to variant calling, ML pipelines, and disease prediction models.

Step 3: Create and contribute

At Georgia Tech, I worked on MoRF prediction using LLMs and BiLSTMs, presented at the Suddath Symposium, and interned at Regeneron on BCR repertoire analysis. These projects taught me to think like a scientist—and engineer solutions that matter.

Advice for newcomers

  • Start with biology or computer science, but explore both.
  • Learn basic genomics, statistics, and data visualization.
  • Get hands-on: build pipelines, join internships, contribute to research.
  • Ask “why” and “how” relentlessly. Curiosity is your compass.

Bioinformatics is the future of personalized medicine, drug discovery, and systems biology. If you're passionate about using AI to decode biology, you're in the right place.

— Aarushi
