About Me

I am a bioinformatics graduate student at Georgia Tech with a strong foundation in machine learning, protein modeling, and personalized medicine. My research focuses on AI-driven approaches to understand molecular recognition features (MoRFs), antibody evolution, and genomic prediction. I'm passionate about building tools that bridge deep learning and molecular biology to accelerate therapeutic discovery and precision medicine.

Research

  • MoRF Binding Propensity & Trigger Extraction (Torres Lab, Georgia Tech)
    NLP + LLM-based mining of MoRF-trigger interactions, coupled with fine-tuned PLM models for residue-level binding prediction.
  • B-cell Repertoire Clustering (Regeneron)
    Graph-based algorithms for identifying clonal lineages and hierarchical structures in VDJ-identical families.
  • Polygenic Risk Scoring and Genomic Imputation (MedGenome)
    Deep ensemble PRS prediction and benchmarking of deep learning-based low-pass WGS imputation workflows.

Selected Projects

  • Disorder-aware Binding Site Predictor – Deep BiLSTM model predicting MoRF-ligand binding residues using ESM2 embeddings and PLM fine-tuning.
  • ALS Variant Discovery Pipeline – Neural network-based GWAS workflow and statistical benchmarking method developed at IIT Mandi.
  • CpG Annotation for Aging – ML workflow for age- and sex-associated CpG loci from methylation data (Mitacs @ UBC).
