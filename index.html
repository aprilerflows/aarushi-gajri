About Me

I am a Master’s student in Bioinformatics at Georgia Tech, with a passion for building AI systems that solve complex biological problems. My research spans protein-ligand interactions, intrinsic disorder modeling, and personalized risk prediction for disease using multi-omics data.

Research Projects

MoRF Trigger NLP

Used LLMs to extract and categorize triggers and secondary structures from MoRF-related abstracts.

Built a comprehensive database of structured MoRF-trigger interactions and structural transitions using prompt engineering and regex parsing. Presented at Suddath Symposium.

MoRF Binding Detector

Dual-head deep learning model predicting ligand binding residues in intrinsically disordered proteins.

Trained BiLSTM and fine-tuned PLM to jointly predict disorder and ligand binding across 8 molecular categories.

BCR Clustering (Regeneron)

Designed novel graph-based pipeline to uncover hierarchies in BCR repertoires with shared VDJ genes.

Inferred affinity maturation trees using Louvain clustering, MST, and local density metrics.

Cardiac PRS (MedGenome)

Built ensemble models for cardiovascular risk using GWAS and boosting-based PRS scoring.

Benchmarked gradient boosting and DL ensembles; evaluated cross-population transferability.

Low-Pass Imputation

Replicated and extended GLIMPSE 2.0 for low-coverage whole genome imputation.

Built DL imputation models and compared performance on simulated low-pass data.

ALS GWAS Pipeline

Deep neural networks + novel statistical test for GWAS without clinical phenotype data.

Demonstrated results on ALS WGS data; method generalizable to multigenic disorders.
